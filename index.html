Ultra-millionaires have advantages over the rest of us

They pay low taxes on their investments

Hawaiʻi is one of only nine states that taxes capital gains at a lower rate than ordinary income—effectively a loophole that almost entirely benefits the wealthy.

$180M/year in new revenue if capital gains were taxed like regular income — with 97% paid by the top 5% of earners, and nothing more from the bottom 80%.

Of taxpayers who reported capital gains in 2019, the 7.7% who earned $400,000+ received 79.4% of all capital gains income in the state. Those making over $1 million a year actually pay a lower effective tax rate than the income group just below them, because Hawaiʻi taxes long-term capital gains at just 7.25% versus the top marginal rate of 11%. If capital gains were taxed like regular income, the state would gain roughly $180 million per year—with 97% paid by the top 5% of earners. The bottom 80% of taxpayers would pay nothing additional.

Source: HI Tax Fairness Coalition — Capital Gains Tax Reform
